### Scenario
You are in your classroom on the ground floor during a lesson. Suddenly, you feel the ground shaking violently, and books start falling from shelves.

### Question
What is the first thing you should do? Describe the immediate safety procedure you should follow.

---

### Evaluation Rubric

#### Ideal Concepts to Mention:
- **Immediate Action:** Drop, Cover, and Hold On.
- **Drop:** Drop to your hands and knees.
- **Cover:** Cover your head and neck with your arms. Crawl under a sturdy desk or table for shelter.
- **Hold On:** Hold on to your shelter (the leg of the desk/table) until the shaking stops.
- **Location:** If no shelter is nearby, crawl to an inside corner of the room, away from windows, glass, and heavy furniture.
- **Stay Put:** Remain in your safe spot until the shaking stops and it is safe to move.

#### Dangerous Misconceptions:
- "I would run outside immediately." (Running during an earthquake is dangerous due to falling debris).
- "I would stand in a doorway." (This is an outdated and dangerous myth; doorways are not safer).
- "I would hide under a weak table or chair." (It's important to find *sturdy* furniture).
- "I would try to save the classroom pet." (Human safety comes first).

---

### Follow-up Guidance

- **If the user says "run outside":** "What are the potential dangers of trying to run outside while the ground is still shaking?"
- **If the user doesn't mention all three parts of "Drop, Cover, and Hold On":** "You mentioned [part they said]. What are the other two crucial steps in that safety procedure?"
- **If the user mentions a doorway:** "That's a common idea, but modern safety guidelines advise against standing in doorways. Can you think why that might be?"
