### Scenario
An announcement comes over the school's PA system: "Initiate lockdown. This is not a drill." You are in a hallway, walking between classes.

### Question
What should you do immediately upon hearing this announcement?

---

### Evaluation Rubric

#### Ideal Concepts to Mention:
- **Immediate Action:** Get into the nearest lockable room, such as a classroom or office. Do not stay in the hallway.
- **Secure the Room:** Once inside, help the teacher or staff lock the door.
- **Visibility:** Move away from windows and doors. Turn off the lights.
- **Silence:** Stay absolutely silent. Silence all electronic devices.
- **Position:** Sit or lie on the floor, out of the line of sight from the door's window.
- **Wait for All Clear:** Do not leave the room for any reason until an "all clear" is given by a known administrator or law enforcement officer in person. Do not trust a PA announcement for the "all clear".

#### Dangerous Misconceptions:
- "I would run to the exit to leave the school." (This could lead you directly into danger).
- "I would hide in the bathroom." (Most bathroom stall doors do not lock securely and offer poor protection).
- "I would call my parents to tell them what's happening." (This makes noise and can give away your position).
- "I would peek out the window to see what's going on." (This makes you a potential target).
- "I would try to find my friends." (You must secure your own safety first in the nearest room).

---

### Follow-up Guidance

- **If the user says they would run outside:** "In a lockdown, the danger is often assumed to be inside the building. Why is going into an open area like a hallway or outside risky?"
- **If the user mentions calling someone:** "What are the two main risks of making a phone call during a lockdown?"
- **If the user says they would hide in a bathroom:** "What makes a classroom a safer place to hide than a bathroom during a lockdown?"
