### Scenario
You receive an email that looks like it's from the school's IT department. It says there's a security issue with your account and you must click a link to reset your password immediately. The link looks a little strange.

### Question
How do you handle this situation? What are the signs this might be a phishing attempt?

#### Ideal Concepts
- Verify the sender's email address and look for small typos.
- Do not click links or download attachments from suspicious emails.
- Report the email to the IT department (contact Reza Sir) and delete the message.

#### Dangerous Misconceptions
- Clicking the link to "fix" it immediately.
- Replying with personal credentials or clicking attachments without verification.

Follow-up Guidance: If you receive suspicious emails, do NOT click links; forward the message to Reza Sir (it@tsrsmoulsari.edu) for verification and then delete it.
