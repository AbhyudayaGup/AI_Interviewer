### Scenario
You receive an email that looks like it's from the school's IT department. It says there's a security issue with your account and you must click a link to reset your password immediately. The link looks a little strange.

### Question
How do you handle this situation? What are the signs this might be a phishing attempt?

---

### Evaluation Rubric

#### Ideal Concepts to Mention:
- **Do Not Click:** The most important step is not to click the link or download any attachments.
- **Verify Sender:** Check the sender's email address carefully. Look for slight misspellings or a non-school domain (e.g., `school-it@gmail.com` instead of `@tsrs.edu.in`).
- **Hover, Don't Click:** Hover your mouse over the link (without clicking) to see the actual URL it leads to. If it's different from what's written, it's a red flag.
- **Look for Red Flags:** Mention signs like a sense of urgency ("do this immediately!"), generic greetings ("Dear Student"), poor grammar, or spelling mistakes.
- **Report:** Report the email to the actual IT department using a known, trusted contact method (e.g., by forwarding it to a specific email address for phishing reports or telling a teacher/staff member).
- **Delete:** After reporting, delete the suspicious email.

#### Dangerous Misconceptions:
- "I would click the link to see where it goes." (This can lead to malware infection or credential theft).
- "I would reply to the email and ask if it's real." (This confirms your email is active to the attacker).
- "I would ignore it and do nothing." (Reporting is crucial to help protect others in the school).
- "If it looks official, it's probably fine." (Attackers are very good at making emails look official).

---

### Follow-up Guidance

- **If the user says they would click the link:** "What could happen if you click on a link in a phishing email, even if you don't enter any information?"
- **If the user suggests ignoring it:** "Why is reporting a suspicious email just as important as not clicking on it?"
- **If the user is unsure what to look for:** "Besides the link, what other parts of an email could you inspect to determine if it's fake?"
